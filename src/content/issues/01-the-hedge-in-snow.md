This article requires a real snowfall and a walk around the hedge behind the
Algoma building.

Snow can reveal passage because compression, displacement, salt, plowing, and
drifting leave marks. It can also hide curbs, holes, property pins, plants,
and surfaces. The future essay should test both effects instead of beginning
from the assumption that winter makes every boundary legible.

## Drafting Rule

Begin with one mark in snow. Follow it only as far as the evidence allows.
Snow does not prove ownership, intention, identity, or permanence. It records
a short interval of pressure and weather.
